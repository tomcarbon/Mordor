The Dark Realm

A land of ash, fire, and shadow stretching from the Ephel Dúath to the Sea of Núrnen

Gorgoroth

The great plateau of Mordor, a vast desolation of ash and slag. Here the Dark Tower of Barad-dûr casts its shadow, and the fires of Mount Doom illuminate the poisoned sky.

Mount Doom

Orodruin, the Fire-mountain. Within its Sammath Naur, the One Ring was forged by Sauron in the fires of the Second Age. Its eruptions darken the skies of all Middle-earth.

Barad-dûr

The Dark Tower. A fortress of iron, built with the power of the One Ring. Its foundations run deep into the rock of Mordor, and at its peak burns the Eye — ever watchful, ever searching.

Ephel Dúath

The Mountains of Shadow. A great fence of jagged peaks forming the western and southern borders of Mordor. Impassable save through Cirith Ungol and the Morgul Vale.

Sea of Núrnen

A vast inland sea in southern Mordor. Its shores are tilled by slaves to feed the armies of the Dark Lord. The only fertile land in all the Black Country.

Minas Morgul

Once Minas Ithil, Tower of the Moon. Captured by the Nazgûl, it became a place of dread — its pale, corpse-light glow visible for miles, a beacon of terror on Mordor's border.

Dark Fortresses

Barad-dûr

The Dark Tower

The greatest fortress ever built in Middle-earth. Founded upon the power of the One Ring in the Second Age, Barad-dûr endured the siege of the Last Alliance for seven years before Sauron was overthrown. Rebuilt in the Third Age as Sauron's power returned, it stands as a monument to domination — a tower of adamant and iron rising above the plateau of Gorgoroth, crowned by the Great Eye wreathed in flame.

  • HeightOver 1,500 feet
  • FoundedSA ~1000
  • LocationGorgoroth Plateau
  • Power SourceThe One Ring

Minas Morgul

Tower of Dark Sorcery

Once the fair tower of Minas Ithil, captured by the Nazgûl in TA 2002. Its walls emit a pale, sickly luminescence — a corpse-light that fills the Morgul Vale with dread. The Witch-king of Angmar rules from its throne, and from its gates march the armies of darkness. The very air around it is poisonous to the living.

  • Original NameMinas Ithil
  • CapturedTA 2002
  • LordThe Witch-king
  • LocationMorgul Vale

Armies of Darkness

The legions that march under the lidless eye

Orcs of Mordor

The backbone of Sauron's army. Bred in the pits beneath Barad-dûr, these creatures know nothing but war and obedience. Tens of thousands strong, they wield crude but effective weapons of iron.

Strength

The Nazgûl

Nine kings of Men, enslaved by the Rings of Power. Neither living nor dead, they are Sauron's most terrible servants. Their fell cry alone can break the will of armies, and their leader is the Witch-king of Angmar.

Terror

Oliphaunts & Haradrim

The Haradrim — Men of the South allied with Sauron. They ride to war upon the great Mûmakil, beasts the size of moving hills, armoured and towering above all other creatures on the field of battle.

Power

Uruk-hai

Greater Orcs — larger, stronger, and able to endure sunlight. Saruman bred his own in Isengard, but Mordor's Black Uruks are older and fiercer. Elite shock troops deployed where the fighting is hardest.

Combat

Trolls of Mordor

Olog-hai — battle trolls bred by Sauron to withstand sunlight. Armoured in black scales and wielding massive hammers, a single troll can smash through a shield wall. They know no fear, only hunger and rage.

Dark Chronicle

SA ~1000

Sauron settles in Mordor

Choosing the land for its natural defenses — mountains on three sides — Sauron begins the construction of Barad-dûr.

SA ~1600

The One Ring is forged

In the Sammath Naur of Mount Doom, Sauron forges the One Ring, pouring his will and power into it to dominate all other ring-bearers.

SA 3434–3441

War of the Last Alliance

Elves and Men lay siege to Barad-dûr. After seven years, Sauron is overthrown when Isildur cuts the Ring from his hand.

TA 2002

Fall of Minas Ithil

The Nazgûl capture Minas Ithil and rename it Minas Morgul — the Tower of Dark Sorcery.

TA 2951

Sauron declares himself

Sauron openly returns to Mordor, rebuilds Barad-dûr, and Mount Doom erupts once more. The Shadow spreads.

TA 3019

The Ring is destroyed

Frodo and Sam enter Mordor. The Ring falls into the fire of Mount Doom. Barad-dûr collapses. Sauron is defeated forever.
